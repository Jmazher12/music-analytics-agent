# Music Genre Characteristics

## Pop
Characterized by catchy melodies, simple chord progressions, and a verse-chorus structure. Typically high danceability (0.5-0.8), moderate to high energy, and high valence. Tempo usually ranges from 100-130 BPM. Often has polished production with emphasis on vocals.

## Rock
Built around electric guitars, bass, and drums. Energy tends to be high (0.6-0.9), with louder overall volume. Danceability varies widely. Subgenres range from soft rock (lower energy, acoustic elements) to heavy metal (very high energy, low valence). Tempo typically 110-140 BPM.

## Hip-Hop / Rap
Features rhythmic vocal delivery over beats. High speechiness (often 0.1-0.4, sometimes higher), strong rhythmic patterns, and emphasis on bass. Danceability is often high (0.6-0.9). Tempo ranges from 70-100 BPM (trap, slower styles) to 90-130 BPM (boom bap, uptempo).

## Electronic / EDM
Produced primarily with electronic instruments and software. Very high danceability and energy. Tempo varies by subgenre: house (120-130 BPM), techno (125-150 BPM), dubstep (140 BPM half-time), drum and bass (160-180 BPM). High instrumentalness is common.

## R&B / Soul
Emphasizes vocal performance, groove, and emotional expression. Moderate to high danceability, moderate energy. Valence varies widely. Often features smooth production, syncopated rhythms, and rich harmonies. Tempo typically 60-110 BPM.

## Jazz
Complex harmonies, improvisation, and swing rhythms. Highly variable across subgenres. Acousticness tends to be high. Instrumentalness can be high (especially in instrumental jazz). Energy and tempo vary widely from slow ballads to uptempo bebop.

## Classical
Orchestral or chamber music with complex compositions. Very high acousticness and instrumentalness. Energy varies from very quiet passages to dramatic fortissimo sections. Speechiness is typically very low. Duration tends to be longer than other genres.

## Country
Features storytelling lyrics, acoustic and steel guitars, fiddle, and banjo. Moderate danceability and energy. Acousticness is often moderate to high. Valence tends to be moderate. Tempo typically 90-140 BPM.

## Latin
Encompasses salsa, reggaeton, bachata, bossa nova, and more. Generally high danceability (0.6-0.9). Reggaeton features distinctive "dembow" rhythm at 90-100 BPM. Latin pop often has high valence and energy.

## Metal
Extreme subgenre of rock with heavy distortion, aggressive vocals, and complex arrangements. Very high energy (0.8-1.0), high loudness, low valence. Tempo varies: thrash metal (180-220 BPM), doom metal (60-80 BPM). Low acousticness and speechiness.

## Folk / Acoustic
Acoustic instruments, storytelling lyrics, and traditional musical forms. High acousticness (0.5-1.0), lower energy, moderate danceability. Often features guitar, banjo, mandolin, or fiddle. Tempo typically moderate (80-130 BPM).

## Reggae
Offbeat rhythmic patterns, bass-heavy arrangements, and laid-back feel. Moderate danceability, moderate energy, typically positive valence. Tempo usually 60-90 BPM. Distinctive rhythmic guitar on the upbeat ("skank").

## Blues
Expressive vocal and guitar work, often with a 12-bar structure. High acousticness in traditional blues, lower in electric blues. Moderate energy, often low valence (melancholic themes). Tempo typically 70-120 BPM.

## Indie
Independent or alternative music that doesn't conform to mainstream trends. Characteristics vary enormously. May have lower popularity scores than mainstream genres despite critical acclaim. Production style ranges from lo-fi to polished.
